**Source:** `workbook_factory/ddg` combined TAM + SAM workbook, branch `promote-sam-findings-round1` (2026-06-30).
**Purpose:** slide-source material — key takeaways, verified headline numbers, and commentary from the DDG-51 supplier-evidence model after this round of research promotion.

**Dollar conventions:** all "$M" are **constant FY2026 dollars** unless noted. "Observed SAM" = reported **first-tier subaward** activity — an *evidence layer*, **not** the full outsourced-market total. Numbers are pulled from the recalculated workbook (LibreOffice, 0 error cells) and the cited research findings.

## 1. The frame — TAM is the market; observed SAM is the evidence layer

**Takeaway:** Keep two denominators separate. TAM sizes the outsourced opportunity top-down; observed SAM shows the *shape* of supplier activity bottom-up. Never present observed SAM as market penetration.

| Denominator | Value | What it supports |
|---|---|---|
| **TAM** (outsourced DDG-51 new-construction opportunity) | **$6,421.7M** cumulative FY22-27 | Market size by FY |
| **Observed SAM** (first-tier subaward evidence) | **≈ $3.1–3.2B** all-time in-corpus | Supplier structure, ship-system mix, hull/lifecycle timing |

**Commentary:** The observed-SAM total is dominated by **HII-Ingalls** first-tier subawards; **GD-BIW first-tier reporting is sparse** (its FY23-27 MYP shows **0 subawards** in-corpus today). So the evidence layer describes the HII supply chain well and the BIW chain barely — a coverage caveat, not a market fact.

## 4. Hull & lifecycle traceability — evidence discipline, not false precision

**Takeaway:** Subaward evidence supports **exact-hull (A/B)** attribution for some rows, **candidate-family (C/D)** for most, and flags **conflicts (X)** — and the model refuses to collapse family dollars onto a single hull.

- Grades: **A/B** = one build hull (direct evidence); **C/D** = candidate hull *set* (never split per hull); **X** = conflict / long-lead / origin-rebuy reference.
- Largest family pools stay family-level by design, e.g. **$850M** HII FY18-22 (7 hulls), **$298M** HII FY13-17 (5 hulls) — timing narrows the set as hulls deliver but never to one hull without direct evidence.
- **DDG 150 call:** the single "DDG 150" Espey subaward is **long-lead for a future (FY28+) hull**, not a build hull of the FY23-27 MYP (which is exactly DDG 140-149). Kept as **X**; DDG 150 recorded only as a low-confidence future-HII hull.

**Commentary:** This is the credibility backbone — the slide message is *"we push hull evidence exactly as far as it goes and no further."* Timing/`Prime Requirement Hull Text` is explicitly **not** used to fake single-hull precision (proven unreliable — it names out-of-family hulls).

## 5. Myth-buster — the "$1.4B missing DDG supplier gap" was an artifact

**Takeaway:** A prior registry suggested ~$1.4B of large DDG suppliers were missing from the model. Verified against the federal awards API — **they aren't DDG suppliers at all.**

| "Missing" vendor | Registry "DDG-signed" | Actual primary customer | NAVSEA (N00024) obligation |
|---|---:|---|---:|
| Major Tool & Machine | $986.9M | **Air Force** (AFLCMC) | **$0.00M** |
| Superior Electromechanical | $146.3M | Navy warfare centers | $0.00M |
| Merrill Technologies | $93.1M | NSWC | $0.00M |
| Seyer Industries | $63.1M | NAVAIR | $0.00M |
| + 3 more (Futuramic, MSM, ACS) | — | Army / DLA | $0.00M |

**Commentary:** The $986.9M was a **contract-ceiling** artifact (Major Tool's top-100 ceiling sum = $50.7B of shared IDIQ ceilings), swept in by a NAICS "tail pass." Realized NAVSEA-shipbuilding obligation for all seven = **$0**. Two lessons for any sizing slide: **ceiling ≠ obligation**, and **NAICS ≠ customer**.

## 6. What changed this round (promotion summary)

**Takeaway:** Four evidence layers were strengthened with cited sources; the build re-verified clean.

- **P3 SWBS:** +55 code mappings (46 observed, 9 curated) → **U00 −$87.3M**, piping/valves tripled.
- **P4 suppliers:** **23** archetype classifications promoted (12 refined + 11 new), reducing the D0/P0 unclassified tail; medium-confidence NAICS-only vendors deferred.
- **P2 hull provenance:** milestones re-cited to primary + S&P Global/IHS **MIRS** registry sources; 4 corrections (2 too-early start-fab dates removed; a launch date reconciled; a christening correctly distinguished from a float-off launch).
- **P1 hull:** DDG 150 marked a future HII (FY28+) hull, low confidence.
- **QA:** build = 37 sheets, **0 recalc error cells**, all reconciliation checks OK, TAM unchanged.

**Commentary (methodology, one line for a backup slide):** the workbook computes these rollups **live** from the transaction evidence, so a single curated-input edit re-flows every dependent view — the map is always internally reconciled to the underlying subawards.

## 7. Caveats to keep on every slide

- **Observed SAM is reported first-tier subawards** — evidence of supplier structure/timing, **not** the full outsourced-market total and **not** market penetration.
- **SWBS coverage is HII-Ingalls only**; BIW is outside the ship-system universe (and under-reports subawards).
- **Constant FY2026$** for SAM rollups; TAM headline is its own basis.
- **~14% of HII observed dollars remain unmapped** (bare work-item codes) — a known, bounded gap.
- **C/D family dollars are never allocated to a single hull** inside the evidence model.
