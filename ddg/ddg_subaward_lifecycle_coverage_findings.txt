================================================================================
DDG-51 SUBAWARD DATA COVERAGE vs. CONSTRUCTION LIFECYCLE
Which hulls do we have a full AP/LLTM-to-delivery subaward history for?
================================================================================
Prepared: 2026-07-01
Scope:    workbook_factory/ddg
Source:   data/workbook_inputs/sam_awards/transactions/ddg_subaward_transactions.csv
          data/workbook_inputs/sam_awards/hull/ddg_hull_master.csv
Companion export: ddg_full_coverage_hulls_subawards.csv (the 358 transaction rows
          for the two full-coverage hulls, all 64 fields)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE QUESTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
For how many DDG-51 hulls do we hold the *full* subaward data history relative to
the construction lifecycle -- i.e. from the AP / long-lead-time-material (LLTM)
phase, through construction and outfit/test, to delivery? Asked first for Flight
III, then for any flight.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
METHOD -- A HULL MUST CLEAR THREE GATES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. COMPLETE LIFECYCLE
   The hull must have actually DELIVERED (Actual delivery date in the past).
   If it has not delivered, the back half of the span has not happened yet.

2. HULL-RESOLVABLE SUBAWARDS
   The subawards must be attributable to that specific hull. In practice only
   HII-Ingalls hulls qualify: General Dynamics / Bath Iron Works (BIW) reports
   subawards at "family" (multi-hull MYP) level, so they cannot be pinned to a
   single hull.

3. UNCLIPPED FRONT (the binding constraint)
   The subaward record only begins Oct 2015 (the FSRS/USASpending pull floor).
   The long-lead (AP/LLTM) phase runs ~2-3 years before start-of-fabrication.
   A hull only has a "full" span if its long-lead phase BEGAN after Oct 2015 --
   otherwise the earliest long-lead subawards predate the data and are missing.

DATA WINDOW ...... Oct 2015  ->  May 2026   (5,006 transaction rows)
LIFECYCLE STAGES . Long-lead (= AP/LLTM, "before fabrication start"),
                   Construction, Outfit / test, Post-delivery
BUILDER SPLIT .... HII-Ingalls: 4,878 rows, 2,007 resolve to a single hull
                   GD-BIW:        128 rows,     5 resolve to a single hull
                   -> BIW hulls are effectively invisible at the hull level.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HEADLINE FINDING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Across ALL flights, exactly TWO hulls have a full AP/LLTM-to-delivery subaward
history that we can resolve to the hull:

        DDG 125  (Flight III, HII-Ingalls)
        DDG 128  (Flight III, HII-Ingalls)

Both are Flight III. No Flight IIA hull qualifies as strictly full, because the
Oct-2015 data floor clips the front (long-lead / early construction) of every
delivered Flight IIA hull.

This answer is the SAME whether or not the post-delivery bucket is required
(see "POST-DELIVERY SENSITIVITY" below). Post-delivery was never the limiter --
the front (long-lead) is.

                     early construction is missing. Only outfit/test and
                     post-delivery survive.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
POST-DELIVERY SENSITIVITY ("what if I don't care about the post-delivery bucket?")
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Still TWO hulls (DDG 125, DDG 128). Removing the post-delivery requirement
relaxes the TAIL of the span, but DDG 119/121/123 fall short at the HEAD (their
long-lead is clipped by the data floor). Dropping the tail requirement cannot
recover missing early long-lead subawards, so the full-span set is unchanged.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHY THE NUMBER DOES NOT GROW BEYOND TWO
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. BIW blind spot  -- every GD/BIW hull (incl. delivered 118/122/124/127) reports
                      subawards at family level; <=1 resolves to a specific hull.
2. Oct-2015 floor  -- clips the long-lead (and sometimes early construction) off
                      every delivered Flight IIA HII hull.
3. Not yet delivered -- everything newer than DDG 128 (129, 131, 133, 135, ...)
                      has long-lead + construction captured but no delivery yet.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CAVEATS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* "$ we have" = hull-resolved DIRECT single-hull attribution. It is the amount we
  can confidently tie to the hull, NOT the hull's total subcontract spend.
* Family-level (shared MYP) subawards that merely name a hull among several
  candidates are excluded; they are an additional, non-hull-specific pool.
* "Long-lead" is the model's AP/LLTM proxy: subawards tagged "before fabrication
  start". Start-fab / launch / delivery dates are from ddg_hull_master.csv.
* DDG 125 is an FY16 ship; a small sliver of its FY14-15 advance procurement
  could in theory predate the Oct-2015 floor, but empirically its first captured
  long-lead subaward is Mar 2017 (17 months clear of the floor), so long-lead is
  well represented and the floor is not clipping it in practice.
