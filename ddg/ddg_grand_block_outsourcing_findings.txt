--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. THE QUESTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Within the "in-build" (non-advance-procurement) subaward spend, the single
largest ship-system bucket was "U00 unmapped" (~$360M). It looked like it might
be dominated by statements of work to fabricate whole chunks of the ship —
e.g. "DDG 137 SOW UNIT OUTSOURCING (GB B15/D52)", "CAPEX: SOW FOR STEELFAB",
"CAPEX: SOW FOR MARMON". The questions: who are these vendors, what is the work,
and do we hold the actual SOW documents?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. CORRECTED BREAKDOWN OF THE $360M "U00 in-build" BUCKET
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The $360M is NOT all whole-unit SOWs. Filtering to the actual SOW / unit /
CAPEX language yields ~$147M, which splits into two very different things
(plus regex noise); the rest is bare-code components:

  ~$55M   Genuine grand-block / hull-unit outsourcing (other shipyards build
          whole ship sections).  <-- the real "outsourced construction labor"
  ~$60M   "CAPEX: SOW FOR [vendor]" — one-time 2021-22 capital SOWs to
          component suppliers (tooling / capacity / qualification).
  ~$213M  Bare work-item-coded components with no descriptive text (the
          unmappable U00 tail) — ordinary parts, not SOWs.
  --------
  ~$360M  total "U00 in-build"

The genuinely "whole chunk of the ship, outsourced during the build" money is
therefore ~$55M, not $360M.

  WHAT IT IS
    HII-Ingalls is overflowing whole hull-unit (grand-block) fabrication to

    other yards fabricate 137's grand blocks before Ingalls starts erecting the
    hull. Classic capacity-surge move.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. DO WE HAVE THE SOW?  ->  NO
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The corpus carries ONLY the one-line FFATA fields:
    - Subaward Description   e.g. "DDG 137 SOW UNIT OUTSOURCING (GB B15)"
    - Description of Requirement (the PRIME's scope) e.g. "CONSTRUCTION OF
      DDG 51 SHIPS FY18-22"
There is NO Statement-of-Work document anywhere in the data. The SOW is a
private HII<->subcontractor contractual document and is not part of public FFATA
subaward reporting. Obtaining the actual scope would require the subcontract
itself (not public) or a FOIA to NAVSEA / the prime. Open sources can only
corroborate that the awards happened (see §7), not reproduce the SOW.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The program and the awards are publicly confirmed (the FFATA data lines up):
  - Gulf Copper: publicly confirmed fabricating "select outfitted structural
    units" at Port Arthur & Galveston, TX, for DDGs 135/137/139 under a 5-YEAR
    STRATEGIC SOURCING AGREEMENT with HII, following a year-long pilot.
      WorkBoat: https://www.workboat.com/gulf-copper-expands-work-with-huntington-ingalls-on-arleigh-burke-class-destroyers
      Port Arthur News: https://panews.com/2025/11/11/gulf-copper-awarded-additional-work-in-support-of-hiis-production-of-u-s-navy-destroyers/
  - Eastern Shipbuilding: confirmed building grand blocks for Ingalls' Flight
    III DDGs; a block of DDG-135 was barged ~170 mi from the FL panhandle to
    Pascagoula; DDG-137/139 in the model. Ship = "about two dozen steel puzzle
    pieces."
      Maritime Executive: https://maritime-executive.com/article/ingalls-subcontracts-out-destroyer-blocks-to-eastern-shipbuilding
  - Broader Navy push: "U.S. Naval Shipyards Accelerating Outsourcing for New
    Construction Programs."
      USNI News: https://news.usni.org/2025/09/12/u-s-naval-shipyards-accelerating-outsourcing-for-new-construction-programs
  - HII strategy detail: outsource the "SIMPLER MODULES," retain "complex hull
    shapes with more sophisticated contents" (incl. the deckhouse) in-house.
      (Maritime Executive, above.)
  - Modular construction background:
      GlobalSecurity: https://www.globalsecurity.org/military/systems/ship/ddg-51-build.htm
      naval-technology: https://www.naval-technology.com/projects/burke/

  CAVEAT — BAE Jacksonville: our FFATA data shows it building GB B15 for
  DDG 137 ($17.7M), but open sources show BAE Jacksonville doing DDG
  REPAIR/MODERNIZATION only, not new-construction block fab. That award rests
  on our FFATA data alone for now (uncorroborated in public reporting).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. THE A/B/C/D GRAND-BLOCK ZONES — NOT PUBLICLY DECODABLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We CANNOT say what A16 / B15 / C15 / D52 map to physically. Reasons:
  - Nothing in the corpus, the SWBS crosswalk, or the module-cost workbook
    decodes them. The block IDs are HII-Ingalls' internal PRODUCT work-breakdown
    (PWBS / erection) coding — orthogonal to our SWBS (functional-system)
    taxonomy. The repo itself notes: "module / grand block / structural unit is
    a physical/production (PWBS)" concept.
  - No open source publishes a decoder (block assignments and terms are
    described as "undisclosed").
  What we can say only as PATTERN (not confirmed): letter + 2-digit (region/band
  + unit position); paired IDs (D41/D42, A16/A21) look like adjacent units; the
  D-series is by far the most outsourced. Decoding the letters would require
  HII build-strategy / erection-sequence drawings (NAVSEA technical records or
  a FOIA).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. CAVEAT — LIFECYCLE-STAGE LABELING OF THE GRAND BLOCKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The grand blocks expose a boundary problem in the "before keel = advance
procurement" heuristic:
  - Under the GROUP-earliest-keel rule (the workbook's Procurement Timing tab),
    they correctly read as IN-BUILD (the FY18-22 block had been building since
    DDG 129's Jan-2021 keel).
  - Under each hull's OWN keel (the materialized Lifecycle Stage), the DDG
    137/139 grand blocks read as "Long-lead" — because 137/139 haven't keel-laid
    yet. That over-counts: grand-block fabrication is CONSTRUCTION, not advance
    material. DDG 135's identical work reads correctly as "Construction" only
    because 135 already keeled. Same activity, different label, purely from the
    (ceremonial) keel-date timing at the prime yard.
  Magnitude is small (~$49M of 137/139 grand blocks), so the AP/LLTM finding
  stands directionally; but grand blocks belong in "early construction," not
  advance procurement.
